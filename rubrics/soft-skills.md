# Soft Skills Rubrics (Consulting Skills)

## Scale Overview
- **1 - Developing**: Learning the skill, inconsistent application
- **2 - Competent**: Applies skill adequately, occasional gaps
- **3 - Proficient**: Consistently demonstrates skill, reliable
- **4 - Advanced**: Excels at skill, role model for others
- **5 - Exceptional**: Mastery level, elevates entire team

---

## COMMUNICATION

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **1** | Struggles to articulate ideas clearly; written/verbal communication needs improvement; may miss context in client interactions |
| **2** | Communicates adequately but sometimes lacks clarity or conciseness; can handle routine client communications |
| **3** | Clear and effective communicator; adapts style to audience; handles client calls professionally; good written documentation |
| **4** | Excellent communicator; leads client presentations confidently; writes compelling proposals; navigates difficult conversations |
| **5** | Exceptional communicator; inspires and influences at executive level; masterful at simplifying complex topics; trusted advisor status |

---

## ORGANIZATION

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **1** | Struggles with task management; often misses deadlines; workspace/projects disorganized; needs frequent reminders |
| **2** | Manages routine tasks adequately; occasionally misses details; organization improving but inconsistent |
| **3** | Well-organized; manages multiple projects effectively; meets deadlines consistently; maintains clean documentation |
| **4** | Highly organized; manages complex workloads seamlessly; proactively identifies risks; helps organize team efforts |
| **5** | Exceptionally organized; creates systems others adopt; manages chaos with ease; brings order to ambiguous situations |

---

## DILIGENCE

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **1** | Work quality inconsistent; requires multiple reviews; may cut corners; needs oversight to ensure completion |
| **2** | Adequate attention to detail; occasional errors; completes work but may miss edge cases |
| **3** | Thorough and reliable; double-checks work; catches own mistakes; consistent quality output |
| **4** | Meticulous attention to detail; goes above standard; anticipates issues before they arise; trusted to own critical work |
| **5** | Exceptional diligence; sets quality standard for team; catches issues others miss; zero-defect mindset |

---

## LEADERSHIP

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **1** | Hesitant to take initiative; waits for direction; uncomfortable leading discussions or decisions |
| **2** | Takes initiative on familiar tasks; can lead small efforts with guidance; developing confidence |
| **3** | Proactively leads projects; makes decisions confidently; takes ownership of outcomes; supports teammates |
| **4** | Strong leader; inspires others; navigates ambiguity well; accountable for team results; develops junior members |
| **5** | Exceptional leader; builds and motivates high-performing teams; influences strategy; sought out for guidance |

---

## VALUE ALIGNMENT

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **1** | Actions sometimes conflict with company values; may prioritize personal interests; cultural fit unclear |
| **2** | Generally aligned with values; occasional lapses; understands expectations but application inconsistent |
| **3** | Consistently demonstrates company values; acts with integrity; good cultural contributor; client-first mindset |
| **4** | Strong values ambassador; models behavior for others; makes decisions aligned with mission; elevates culture |
| **5** | Exceptional values alignment; defines and shapes culture; inspires others through example; embodies company mission |

---

## LeanScale Core Values Reference

When evaluating Value Alignment, consider alignment with:

1. **Client Success First** — Prioritize client outcomes over internal convenience
2. **Speed & Quality** — Move fast without sacrificing excellence  
3. **Ownership** — Take full responsibility for outcomes
4. **Transparency** — Communicate openly, even when difficult
5. **Continuous Improvement** — Always learning, always getting better
